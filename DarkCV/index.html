Child Labour Project

Child Labour: Causes, Effects & Solutions

A simple project page presenting key facts, real-world images, causes, impacts and actionable solutions to reduce child labour.

Learn more

Project Description

This project explains what child labour is, why it happens, who is affected and what can be done to prevent it. The page contains short explanations, a small image gallery, statistics and recommended actions for communities, schools and governments.

What is child labour?

Child labour refers to the employment of children in any work that deprives them of their childhood, potential, and dignity, and that is harmful to their physical and mental development. It includes hazardous work as well as work that interferes with schooling.

Main Causes

  • Poverty and low household income
  • Limited access to quality education
  • Cultural norms and traditional roles
  • Weak labour laws and enforcement
  • Demand for cheap labour in informal sectors

Effects of Child Labour

Child labour has severe short- and long-term consequences — it reduces educational attainment, causes health problems, and perpetuates cycles of poverty across generations.

Solutions & Recommendations

  1. Improve access to free, quality education and school feeding programs.
  2. Strengthen social protection for vulnerable households.
  3. Enforce labour laws and remove children from hazardous work.
  4. Raise public awareness and change social norms through community programs.
  5. Support livelihoods for families (microfinance, training).

Conclusion

Ending child labour requires combined action from families, communities, civil society and governments. Small local projects — like running catch-up classes, creating child protection committees and supporting families — can make a major difference.

Quick Facts

Children under 14 are often employed in informal, unregulated work. Many are denied education.
